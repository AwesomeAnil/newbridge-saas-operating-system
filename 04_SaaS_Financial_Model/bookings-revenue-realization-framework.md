# 📘 Bookings → Revenue Realization Framework (B2RRF)

## Business Excellence Operating Model



\---

# 1\. 🎯 Executive Summary



A high-performing SaaS organization must achieve both:



|Layer|Target|Purpose|
|-|-|-|
|Sales|Bookings Target (ACV / ARR / TCV)|Quota attainment|
|Finance|Revenue Target (Recognized FY)|P\&L / Budget attainment|



**Success Definition:**

VP Success = Hit Bookings Target + Hit Revenue Target



\---

# 2\. ⚠️ The Naïve Model (What Fails)



|Practice|Problem|
|-|-|
|Bookings-only focus|Ignores revenue timing|
|Revenue-only focus|Ignores pipeline health|
|Weighted pipeline|False precision, unreliable forecasts|
|No bridge between metrics|Sales ≠ Finance misalignment|



**Outcome:**

* Strong bookings but missed revenue
* Late surprises and poor forecast accuracy



\---

# 3\. ✅ The Governed Model (B2RRF)



## 3.1 Core Principle



|Principle|Description|
|-|-|
|Bookings ≠ Revenue|Different timing and purpose|
|Revenue is time-dependent|Driven by deal timing|
|Pipeline uncertainty must be explicit|Use risk states, not probabilities|
|Coverage must be empirical|Based on historical conversion|
|Geo accountability required|Each region behaves differently|



\---

## 3.2 Bookings → Revenue Bridge



|Component|Description|
|-|-|
|Opening ARR Contribution|Revenue from existing contracts|
|In-Year Bookings Contribution|Revenue from new deals (IYRC)|
|Churn / Contraction|Lost revenue from cancellations or downsell|
|Expansion (Timed)|Upsell / cross-sell realized within the year|



**Formula:**
FY Revenue
= Opening ARR

* \+ In-Year Bookings Contribution
− Churn
± Expansion



\---

# 4\. 🔗 In-Year Revenue Contribution (IYRC)



|Variable|Description|
|-|-|
|ACV|Annual Contract Value|
|Close Month|Month deal is signed|
|Months Remaining|Months left in fiscal year|
|IYRC|ACV × (Months Remaining / 12)|

\---

## Example



|Deal|ACV|Close Month|IYRC|
|-|-|-|-|
|A|120K|Jan|60K|
|B|120K|Apr|30K|
|C|120K|Jun|10K|



**Insight:** Same ACV → different revenue impact



\---

# 5\. 📊 Planning Model



## Step-by-Step

|Step|Description|
|-|-|
|1|Define Revenue Target|
|2|Calculate Opening ARR|
|3|Subtract Churn|
|4|Add Expansion|
|5|Compute Revenue Gap|
|6|Apply Proration|
|7|Derive Required Bookings|



\---

## Revenue Gap



|Component|Formula / Description|
|-|-|
|Revenue Target|FY financial goal|
|Opening ARR|Starting recurring revenue|
|Churn|Expected revenue loss|
|Expansion|Expected upsell|
|Revenue Gap|Target − (Opening ARR − Churn + Expansion)|



\---

## Example



Revenue Target = 100M

Opening ARR = 70M

Churn = -10M

Expansion = +10M



Revenue Gap = 30M

Required Bookings = 30M / 0.5 = 60M





\---



# 6\. 📈 Coverage Framework



| Metric             | Formula                                      | Target      |

|-------------------|----------------------------------------------|------------|

| Bookings Coverage | Pipeline ACV / Bookings Target               | ≥ 3.0×     |

| Revenue Coverage  | Pipeline IYRC / Revenue Gap                 | ≥ 1.5×     |



\---



## Why 1.5× Revenue Coverage?



| Concept | Formula |

|--------|--------|

| ECR (Conversion Rate) | Actual Revenue / Pipeline IYRC |

| Required Coverage | 1 / ECR |



Example:

ECR = 65% → Coverage ≈ 1.5×

# 8\. ⚠️ Risk-Based Deal State Model



|Deal State|Definition|Risk Level|
|-|-|-|
|Committed|Confirmed close, minimal blockers|Low|
|Likely|Strong progression, dependencies exist|Medium|
|Uncommitted|Early stage, weak intent|High, not in forecast|
|At Risk|Known blockers impacting closure|Very High|
|Pushed|Slipping beyond planned close period|Extreme, remove from forecast|



\---

## Risk Reasoning Structure



|Field|Description|
|-|-|
|Primary Risk Reason|Main blocker affecting deal|
|Secondary Risk|Additional contributing risk|
|Risk Impact|Timing / Value / Both|



\---

# 9\. 🔮 Scenario Forecasting



|Scenario|Included Deal States|
|-|-|
|Conservative|Committed|
|Most Likely|Committed + Likely|
|Aggressive|All except Pushed|
